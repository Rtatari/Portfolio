What is it?

Ciconia is a minimalist table lamp that can be lowered or raised, or angled to fit the user’s tastes. This lamp combines wood and plaster to create a design product which incorporates adjustability with a minimal amount of pieces. In addition, the lamp’s interaction uses a dimmer to turn on and off the lamp and control the brightness, and uses a LED strip to project light in a concentrated area. Its smaller size as a desk lamp allows for effortless portability.

Persona

Jennifer is a 26 year old graduate student in SIAT at SFU. She is very busy studying for her courses and also with her teacher assistant position for IAT 309, a writing course which includes marking a lot of papers. She enjoys reading books in her spare time, therefore the lamp should be adjustable and is easy to move around to her different reading spots. Jennifer also does her TA work at night most of the time so she needs a table lamp that provides a comfortable brightness level for her needs.

Form & Process

We first found inspiration in simple shapes, and aimed to establish a minimal lamp that would be clear to use. From initial iterations, the form was not dynamic, therefore we explored angles and adjusted our base shape. The exposed wire shows clarity and honesty to the table lamp. Our final form resembles a crane, but holds a light at the end of its horizontal arm.

Technology

To turn on and off the Ciconia lamp, we used a LED dimmer and the user can also use the dimmer to adjust the brightness of the light. We used a LED strip for our light source because it is energy efficient and flexible. To power the lamp, we used a plug-in power adapter because the user may need to use the lamp for a long time and it is a convenient way of getting electricity.
